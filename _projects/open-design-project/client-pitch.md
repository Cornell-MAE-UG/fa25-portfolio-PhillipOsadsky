# The Lanternfly Terminator  
**Team Bug-anators**  
**Client(s): Cornell CALS Extension / E&J Gallo Winery / National Grape**

---

## Problem

Grape farms supplying wineries and juice processors are increasingly impacted by Spotted Lanternflies (SLFs) during the growing and harvesting season.  

A Penn State–Cornell study found that more than **60% of SLFs were harvested directly into 1.5-ton grape bins** (up to 80% in some rows). Under current quality standards, a juice load may be rejected if just **1–2 insect fragments are detected in a 1,000-gram core sample**.  

The grape vines also lose vitality after the SLF attaches to the vines. While current strategies focus on applying pesticides, they are too expensive as a constant preventive measure.  

Our plan focuses specifically on stopping adult SLFs from reaching and settling on vines by **intercepting them at key hotspots and entry points in the vineyard**.

---

## Impact

The NY grape industry is valued at **$16.81 billion annually**, but the increase in SLF infestations causes a significant decrease in this number.  

By keeping SLFs off grapevines pre-harvest, we reduce infestations. Once SLFs land on vines, they are difficult to control across an area and can rapidly degrade the quality of the harvest.  

Focusing on this issue lessens SLFs' impact on:
- Product safety  
- Taste  
- Economic value of harvested grapes  

---

## Proposed Direction  
### SLF Automated Scent Trap

Our concept is a **scented (Tree of Heaven, etc.) SLF trap** that attracts and eliminates them via:
- Electric grid  
- Adhesive  
- Mechanical suction trap  

The device will be:
- Weather-proof  
- Self-sufficient (power supply + SLF removal)  

The SLF would be collected in a section that users can empty and replace.

---

### Mechanism

1. Set up trap at vineyard hotspot  
2. Deploy scent mechanism  
3. Scent lures SLFs away from grapevines  
4. Trap captures and eliminates SLFs  

---

### Advantages

- Less costly than pesticides  
- Avoids repeated harvester tool changes  

---

## Validation Plan

By the semester's end, we aim to validate the functionality of our **auto scent dispersion and elimination methods** by testing flying objects similar to SLFs to achieve the desired response from the mechanism.

---

## Unknowns

**Ecological Impact (Non-target species)**  
Our device may attract and harm pollinators and beneficial predators.

*Mitigation:*  
- Use scent minimizing pollinator attraction  
- Conduct small-scale field testing  

---

**Maintenance & Usability**  
Devices require routine servicing across large vineyard areas.

*Mitigation:*  
- Increase storage capacity  
- Reduce maintenance frequency  

---

## Questions for Clients

- Is this design worth pursuing given the complexity of SLF attraction?  
- Could this design meaningfully reduce SLF presence on grape vines?  
- What scents attract SLFs more effectively than grape vines while minimizing harm to pollinators?  
- How can vineyard SLF hotspots be reliably identified?  
- Can existing Tree of Heaven hotspots be used instead of deploying scent mechanisms?
